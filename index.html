Biography

Dr. Titu Samson is an accomplished Electrical and Radio Frequency (RF) Engineer with over a decade of experience in electrical systems design, RF engineering, and multidisciplinary project collaboration. Currently serving at the School of Earth and Space Exploration at Arizona State University, he specializes in designing and developing advanced electrical systems—including sensor integration and programming of control devices based on sensor data. Dr. Samson has led and contributed to projects involving electrical and RF design for CubeSat payloads and radar systems, automating testing and data acquisition processes using Python and SCPI commands to enhance efficiency and accuracy. His expertise extends to RF systems, antenna design, amplifier design, EMI mitigation, DC-DC converters, Arduino integration, and I2C sensor programming, utilizing tools like AWR, Altium, and LTspice for circuit design, simulation, and testing.

Previously, as an Antenna Engineer at the Advanced Center for Atmospheric Radar Research in India, Dr. Samson designed and simulated phased array antennas and developed multilayer PCBs for radar systems. He holds a PhD in Electrical Engineering from Cochin University of Science and Technology, where his thesis focused on the design, development, calibration, and validation of a 205 MHz Wind Profiler Radar. Proficient in C, Python, and MATLAB, he brings strong problem-solving skills and clear technical communication to his collaborative work with cross-functional teams.
